About

I design at the intersection of what people feel, what systems allow, and what business needs.

💗

What people feel

Emotion drives behavior long before logic does. I lean on interviews, ethnography, and behavioral research to understand the messy human side — what people trust, what frustrates them, what they'll actually do (not just what they say).

Hover or tap each phrase to see how I think about it.

Background

I grew up between Taipei and the U.S., which taught me early that the same interface can mean very different things depending on context, culture, and habit. That curiosity led me to the University of Michigan, where I studied Cognitive Science and Psychology with a Decision focus and a minor in User Experience.

My research background in behavioral decision-making — studying present bias and loss aversion in sick-leave choices — shaped how I think about design: people don't always act rationally, but their behavior is predictable if you look closely. Good design meets them where they are.

Now, as an MHCI candidate at Carnegie Mellon, I'm deepening my craft in research, strategy, and interaction design while continuing to work across product management, consulting, and in-vehicle experience research.

Why Strategy & UI/UX?

I like clarity

The best strategy work makes messy problems legible. I enjoy taking competing priorities, scattered research, and ambiguous constraints and turning them into a clear point of view.

I care about impact

UI/UX is where intent becomes behavior. A small change in navigation, wording, or feedback can change whether someone completes a task, trusts a product, or feels included.

I love the in-between

My sweet spot sits between research and design, between business goals and user needs. That's where the most interesting problems live.
